# SiteLaunch — Product Brief

## One-liner
SiteLaunch is a white-label client onboarding platform that turns the messy "send me your content" phase of web projects into a guided, branded experience.

## What it does
When a web agency or freelancer wins a new website project, the first bottleneck is always the same: getting content, credentials, brand assets, and approvals from the client. SiteLaunch replaces the back-and-forth email chains, shared Google Docs, and lost attachments with a single, step-by-step submission flow that lives on the agency's own subdomain.

Agencies sign up, create a partner workspace, build a custom intake form using a drag-and-drop editor, and send their client a branded link. The client walks through a conversational, guided experience — one step at a time — uploading logos, filling in copy, providing logins, and signing off. The agency gets notified the moment everything is submitted, and all files and data land in one organized dashboard.

## Who it's for
Web design agencies, freelance designers, marketing studios, and any service business that needs to collect structured information and files from clients before kicking off a project. The platform is multi-tenant, so larger agencies can create sub-partner accounts for internal teams or white-label it for their own clients.

## Core capabilities
- Multi-step form builder with drag-and-drop field ordering (Gravity Forms-style UI)
- White-label client portal on custom subdomains (agency.mysitelaunch.com or custom domains)
- File uploads with organized storage per submission
- Real-time email notifications for both agency and client
- Multi-tenant account hierarchy (agency > sub-partners > clients)
- Self-serve signup with usage-based billing tiers

## The feeling
SiteLaunch should feel modern, confident, and effortless — like a product built by designers, for designers. The client-facing experience is warm and conversational, never clinical or intimidating. Think: the polish of Linear meets the approachability of Typeform. The dashboard is clean and functional, not cluttered. The brand should communicate professionalism and trust — this is a tool agencies put their own name on, so it needs to look premium enough to sit alongside their brand.

## Keywords / mood
Professional, clean, trustworthy, modern, approachable, streamlined, white-label, premium-but-not-stuffy, designer-friendly, organized, calm.

## Color direction
The product itself uses a neutral slate/gray palette with the partner's brand color as the accent. Branding for SiteLaunch as a company should feel distinct from any one partner — consider deep navy, warm neutrals, or a sophisticated single-accent color that signals reliability without competing with client brands.

## Logo considerations
The name "SiteLaunch" suggests momentum and beginnings. The logo should work well at small sizes (favicons, nav bars), in both light and dark contexts, and alongside partner logos without overpowering them. A wordmark with a subtle icon or ligature treatment would work well. Avoid anything that feels too "techy" or startup-y — this is a tool for creative professionals.

## Competitive context
Alternatives include Content Snare, Copilot, Dubsado intake forms, and manual processes (email + Google Drive). SiteLaunch differentiates through its white-label multi-tenant model, the drag-and-drop form builder, and the conversational client experience that makes the intake feel guided rather than bureaucratic.

## Tech stack (for context)
Next.js, Supabase, Vercel, Resend. Hosted at mysitelaunch.com with wildcard subdomains for each partner.
